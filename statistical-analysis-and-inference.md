# Statistical Analysis and Inference

Bioinformatics relies heavily on statistics to interpret biological data, distinguish signal from noise, and make predictions.

## Probability Theory

*   **Random Variables**: Discrete vs. Continuous.
*   **Probability Distributions**:
    *   Binomial Distribution
    *   Poisson Distribution
    *   Normal (Gaussian) Distribution
    *   Negative Binomial Distribution (crucial for RNA-Seq)

## Hypothesis Testing

*   **Null and Alternative Hypotheses ($H_0$ vs $H_1$)**.
*   **P-values**: Definition and interpretation.
*   **Multiple Testing Correction**:
    *   Bonferroni correction.
    *   False Discovery Rate (FDR) and Benjamini-Hochberg procedure.

## Statistical Inference

*   **Estimation**: Point estimates and Confidence Intervals.
*   **Likelihood**: Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE).
*   **Bayesian Inference**: Priors, Posteriors, and Bayes' Theorem.

## Analysis with R

R is the standard language for statistical analysis in bioinformatics.

*   Data manipulation with `dplyr`.
*   Visualization with `ggplot2`.
*   Bioconductor packages for genomic data.
